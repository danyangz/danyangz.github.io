Biography

I am an assistant professor in the Department of Computer Science at Duke University. Before Duke, I was a postdoctoral researcher at UC Berkeley, working with Professor Ion Stoica. I completed my Ph.D. in the Paul G. Allen School of Computer Science and Engineering at University of Washington, advised by Professor Tom Anderson and Professor Arvind Krishnamurthy. My research has received FAST Best Paper Award (2021), Amazon Research Award (2021), IBM Academic Award (2021), and Meta Research Award (2022, 2021).

Research

My research focuses on the following two directions:

  • Datacenter and Cloud Computing
    • Remote Direct Memory Access [NSDI23, NSDI22]
    • Differentially Oblivious Databases [VLDB23]
    • Cloud Network Abstractions [NSDI22]
    • In-Memory Object Stores [VLDB22]
    • Serverless Systems [SIGCOMM21]
    • Safe Language for Kernel Development [FAST21]
    • Network Function Virtualization [SIGCOMM20, NSDI20]
    • Container Networking [NSDI19]
    • Datacenter Networks [SIGCOMM17, NSDI17]
  • Machine Learning Systems
    • Serving ML Workflows on the Edge [VLDB23]
    • Model Training with All Parallelisms [OSDI22]
    • Graph Neural Networks [AAAI22]
    • Token-Level Pipeline Parallelism [ICML21]
    • Privacy-Preserving Machine Learning [ICLR21]
    • Tensor Program Generation [OSDI20]
