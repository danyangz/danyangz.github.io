Biography

I am an assistant professor in the Department of Computer Science at Duke University. Before Duke, I was a postdoctoral researcher at UC Berkeley, working with Professor Ion Stoica. I completed my Ph.D. in the Paul G. Allen School of Computer Science and Engineering at University of Washington, advised by Professor Tom Anderson and Professor Arvind Krishnamurthy. My research has received NSF CAREER Award (2023), IEEE/ACM MICRO Best Paper Award (2025), USENIX Security Distinguished Paper Award (2023), USENIX FAST Best Paper Award (2021), Google Academic Research Award (2024), Meta Research Award (2022, 2021), Amazon Research Award (2021), and IBM Academic Award (2021).

Research

My research mainly spans two threads: (1) datacenter networking: RDMA, collective communication, application networks; (2) GPU systems: training, inference, multiplexing.

  • Networking
    • Remote Direct Memory Access (RDMA): Harmonic (NSDI24), Husky (NSDI23), Collie (NSDI22).
    • Collective Communication: MCCS (SIGCOMM24), NetHint (NSDI22), Hoplite (SIGCOMM21).
    • Remote Procedure Call (RPC) and Application Networking: ADN (NSDI25), mRPC (NSDI23).
    • Software-Hardware Co-Design: HAL (ISCA24), Gallium (SIGCOMM20).
  • GPUs
    • Training: Phantora (NSDI26), AXLearn (MLSys26), Alpa (OSDI22), TeraPipe (ICML21).
    • Inference: KVCOMM (NeurIPS25), Plato (COLM25), VTC (OSDI24), Punica (MLSys24), JellyBean (VLDB23).
    • Kernels & Compilers: FlashSVD (AAAI26), Ansor (OSDI20).
    • Multiplexing: Nixie (OSDI26).
    • Compression: LLM.265 (MICRO25).
  • Side interest: applying programming language and formal methods for system software.
    • Rust for Operating Systems: Enoki (EuroSys24), Bento (FAST21).
    • SMT-based Software Verification: Lightning (VLDB22), Gravel (NSDI20).
